How the score works — The Heat Index is a composite of three independent signals, each scored 0–100 (0 = deep value / buy, 100 = overheated / sell) and weighted together:

① 200-day MA Position (40%) — How far today's price sits below or above its 200-day moving average. Price deeply below the MA = historically oversold territory. Price well above = extended. This is the cleanest cycle-positioning signal because it has no secular drift and has been reliable across every crypto cycle.

② Protocol Revenue Yield (30%) — The 30-day average of daily fees paid to Solana divided by SOL price. Think of it as a P/E ratio for the network: high yield means the network is generating lots of fee revenue per dollar of SOL price = fundamentally cheap. Low yield means the price has run ahead of actual on-chain demand.

③ TVL / Price (30%) — Solana DeFi TVL (in billions USD) divided by SOL price. High ratio = lots of on-chain activity per dollar of SOL = constructive. Known limitation: during the 2022–2023 bear market, TVL collapsed with price (FTX wiped the ecosystem), making this signal unreliable in isolation. At 30% weight it adds useful information without dominating the composite.

Data: DefiLlama (TVL + fees) · CoinCap (price). Updated daily. Not financial advice — always combine with macro context before acting.
